💡 answer:Let B_M be the amount Terry spent on breakfast on Monday.Let B_T be the amount Terry spent on breakfast on Tuesday.Let B_W be the amount Terry spent on breakfast on Wednesday.From the problem statement, we have the following information:B_M = 6B_T = twice as much on Tuesday as on Monday, so B_T = 2 * B_M = 2 * 6 = 12.On Wednesday Terry spent a certain multiple of what he did the previous two days combined.Let the multiple be 'm'.The amount spent on the previous two days combined is B_M + B_T = 6 + 12 = 18.So, B_W = m * (B_M + B_T) = m * 18.Terry spent a total of 54.Total spent = B_M + B_T + B_W = 54.Substitute the values we have:6 + 12 + B_W = 5418 + B_W = 54B_W = 54 - 18B_W = 36We also have B_W = m * 18.So, 36 = m * 18.To find the multiple 'm', we divide both sides by 18:m = 36 / 18m = 2The question asks for the ratio of the amount Terry spent on Wednesday to the amount he spent on the previous two days combined.Amount spent on Wednesday = B_W = 36.Amount spent on the previous two days combined = B_M + B_T = 18.Ratio = (Amount spent on Wednesday) / (Amount spent on the previous two days combined)Ratio = B_W / (B_M + B_T)Ratio = 36 / 18Ratio = 2We can also use 'm' to directly find the ratio.We know that B_W = m * (B_M + B_T).So, the ratio B_W / (B_M + B_T) = m.We found m = 2.Therefore, the ratio is 2.Let's double check.Monday: 6Tuesday: 2 * 6 = 12Previous two days combined: 6 + 12 = 18Wednesday: m * 18Total: 6 + 12 + m * 18 = 5418 + m * 18 = 54m * 18 = 54 - 18m * 18 = 36m = 36 / 18 = 2Wednesday spent = m * (previous two days combined) = 2 * 18 = 36.Total spent = Monday + Tuesday + Wednesday = 6 + 12 + 36 = 18 + 36 = 54.This matches the total amount given in the problem.The ratio of the amount Terry spent on Wednesday to the amount he spent on the previous two days combined is:Ratio = (Amount spent on Wednesday) / (Amount spent on Monday and Tuesday)Ratio = 36 / (6 + 12)Ratio = 36 / 18Ratio = 2
