question:What is the speed of the stream if a canoe rows upstream at 9 km/hr and downstream at 12 km/hr?

answer:Let v_c be the speed of the canoe in still water and v_s be the speed of the stream.When the canoe rows upstream, the effective speed is the speed of the canoe minus the speed of the stream, because the stream is working against the canoe.So, the upstream speed is v_c - v_s.We are given that the upstream speed is 9 km/hr.So, v_c - v_s = 9 (Equation 1)When the canoe rows downstream, the effective speed is the speed of the canoe plus the speed of the stream, because the stream is helping the canoe move faster.So, the downstream speed is v_c + v_s.We are given that the downstream speed is 12 km/hr.So, v_c + v_s = 12 (Equation 2)We have a system of two linear equations with two variables, v_c and v_s:1) v_c - v_s = 92) v_c + v_s = 12We want to find the speed of the stream, which is v_s.We can solve this system of equations using either substitution or elimination method.Let's use the elimination method.Add equation (1) and equation (2):(v_c - v_s) + (v_c + v_s) = 9 + 12v_c - v_s + v_c + v_s = 212v_c = 21v_c = 21 / 2 = 10.5 km/hrNow substitute the value of v_c into equation (2) to find v_s:v_c + v_s = 1210.5 + v_s = 12v_s = 12 - 10.5v_s = 1.5 km/hrAlternatively, we could subtract equation (1) from equation (2) to directly solve for v_s:(v_c + v_s) - (v_c - v_s) = 12 - 9v_c + v_s - v_c + v_s = 32v_s = 3v_s = 3 / 2 = 1.5 km/hrBoth methods give the same value for v_s.The speed of the stream is 1.5 km/hr.We can check the answer.If the speed of the stream is 1.5 km/hr and the speed of the canoe in still water is 10.5 km/hr.Upstream speed = v_c - v_s = 10.5 - 1.5 = 9 km/hr.
